Shri Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga Temple,Nashik, Maharashtra

Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga, situated on the banks of the Godavari River in Nashik, Maharashtra, is considered the most renowned among the twelve Jyotirlingas. This Jyotirlinga stands out as the only abode where three Shiva Lingas are consecrated in one place. These three Shiva Lingas are considered representations of Tridev, Lord Brahma, Lord Vishnu and Lord Shiva. Near the temple, the sacred Godavari River emerges from the Brahmagiri mountain. According to mythological stories, in ancient times, Rishi Gautam lived on the Brahmagiri mountain with his wife, Ahilya. Other Rishis became jealous of Rishi Gautam and falsely accused him of Gau Hatya (killing a cow). To attain redemption from this sin, the other Rishis asked him to bring the river Ganga to this place. Since it was not possible for Maa Ganga to come to Earth, Rishi Gautam established a Shiv Linga and began worshipping it.

Impressed by the sincere devotion of Rishi Gautam, Lord Shiva appeared before him. Lord Shiva asked Sage Gautama to make a wish, and he requested the descent of the river Ganga to this place. Devi Ganga agreed to fulfil the sage's request on the condition that she would only come when Lord Mahadev resided in that place. Accepting Ganga's wish, Lord Shiva manifested himself as the Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga. Eventually, the Ganga River flowed there in the form of the Godavari River. Godavari is also known as Dakshin Ganga. It is believed that by visiting Trimbakeshwar Jyotirlinga, one's wishes are fulfilled, and they attain liberation from sins committed in this and past lives. Devotees also worship Lord Shiva here to alleviate financial issues and negative energies.
